Q & A

Everything you need to know

What is the dress code?+
Garden party chic — think flowing, romantic, and comfortable enough for cobblestone paths. We encourage soft florals, linen suits, and anything that makes you feel like you belong in the Italian countryside. Please avoid white, ivory, or champagne out of respect for the bride.
What should I wear to the Welcome BBQ on April 18?+
The Welcome BBQ theme is Something Blue Before I Do — wear your best shade of blue! Think navy linen, dusty blue sundresses, powder blue blazers, or any hue of blue you love. It's a fun, festive way to kick off the weekend. Smart casual to cocktail attire works perfectly.
Will the ceremony be indoors or outdoors?+
The ceremony will be held outdoors in the gardens of Casale Doria Pamphilj, a breathtaking 17th-century estate in the Roman countryside. The reception dinner will move inside to the grand hall. April in Rome is typically warm and sunny — expect highs of 65–72°F.
Do I need a passport? Any visa info?+
Yes — make sure your passport is valid for at least 6 months beyond April 20, 2027. US citizens do not need a visa for stays under 90 days in Italy. Book flights and accommodations as early as possible. Check your government's travel advisory site for the most current entry requirements.
Is there transportation from Rome to the venue?+
Yes! We will be organizing a shuttle from central Rome to the Casale on both April 18 (Welcome BBQ) and April 19 (Wedding Day). Details and booking info will be sent closer to the date. The Casale is approximately 22km from central Rome — about a 30-minute drive. Return shuttles run at midnight.
Can I take photos during the ceremony?+
We're asking guests to keep the ceremony phone-free so you can be fully present in the moment — and so our photographer can capture you without screens in the way. Feel free to snap away during the cocktail hour and reception. Our hashtag is #OliviaAndEthan2027.
When should I book flights and hotels?+
As soon as possible! Spring in Rome is peak travel season. We recommend booking by September 2026 at the latest. Check our Travel page for hotel recommendations near the venue, plus tips for extending your stay in Rome. April in Italy is magical — make a full trip of it!
What is the RSVP deadline?+
Please RSVP by February 1st, 2027. This gives us enough time to finalize catering, seating, and logistics with the venue. Head to the RSVP page to submit your response — we can't wait to celebrate with you!

Travel

Everything you need to plan your Roman holiday

Important: Spring in Rome is popular — we recommend booking flights and hotels by September 2026 to get the best rates. The venue is located in Fiumicino, about 22km from central Rome (~30-minute drive).

Getting There

Flying In

The closest airport is Rome Fiumicino (FCO) — Leonardo da Vinci International Airport, just 5km from the Casale. Direct flights from most major US cities are available via ITA Airways, Delta, and United.

Shuttle Service

We will arrange a private shuttle from central Rome on both April 18 (Welcome BBQ) and April 19 (Wedding Day). Booking details will be sent to all confirmed guests. Return shuttles run at midnight.

By Car / Taxi

Taxis and rideshares (FREE NOW, Uber) are readily available from Rome. The drive from central Rome is approximately 30–40 minutes. Casale address: Via Onorato Occioni, 12, 00054 Fiumicino.

Train + Transfer

The Leonardo Express runs between Roma Termini and Fiumicino Airport (€14, 32 min). From Fiumicino, a short taxi to the Casale is the easiest option. Scenic and straightforward.

Where to Stay

At the Casale

Casale Doria Pamphilj has 25 independent apartments on site — the best option for the full experience. Book directly at casaledoriapamphilj.it. Mention Olivia & Ethan's wedding when booking!

In Fiumicino

Several hotels are within 10 minutes of the venue. We recommend the Hilton Rome Airport or NH Hotel Rome Airport for convenience, or explore charming B&Bs in the Fiumicino town center.

In Central Rome

If you'd like to base yourself in Rome and use our shuttle, we love the Trastevere and Campo de' Fiori neighborhoods — charming, walkable, and full of incredible food and history.

Extend Your Trip

Rome deserves more than a weekend! We highly encourage you to arrive early or stay after. Head to our Things to Do page for our personal recommendations around the city and beyond.

Things to Do

Our personal guide to Rome & beyond

Rome is a city that rewards the wanderer. These are our favorite spots — the places we've fallen in love with on our own trips. We hope you fall for them too.

Must-See

The Colosseum

Book tickets in advance — the line without a booking can be hours. Spring light makes the stone glow golden. Go at opening time for the best experience.

Must-See

Trastevere

Our favorite neighborhood. Cobblestone streets, ivy-covered buildings, and the best aperitivo bars in the city. Perfect for an evening wander and dinner.

Art & Culture

Borghese Gallery

Book well in advance — entry is strictly timed. Bernini's sculptures are among the most breathtaking things you'll ever see in person. Allow two hours minimum.

Food & Drink

Roscioli

The best cacio e pepe in Rome, full stop. Also a wine bar and deli. Make a reservation — it books up weeks ahead. Worth every bit of the wait.

Food & Drink

Gelato at Fatamorgana

Artisanal gelato with unusual flavors like basil, ginger, and pink pepper. Multiple locations across Rome — don't skip this one.

Food & Drink

Campo de' Fiori Market

Every morning there's a lively market — grab fresh fruit, local produce, and flowers. Then settle into one of the surrounding bars for a proper espresso.

Day Trip

Ostia Antica

An ancient Roman port city just 30 minutes from Rome — and far less crowded than Pompeii. Incredibly preserved with stunning mosaics and open-air ruins.

Day Trip

Frascati Wine Region

30 minutes from Rome in the Castelli Romani hills. Rolling vineyards, panoramic views, and the local white wine is delightful. Perfect for a half-day escape.

Hidden Gem

Aventine Keyhole

Peek through the Knights of Malta keyhole for a perfectly framed view of St. Peter's Dome. Free, quick, and utterly magical. Don't miss it.
