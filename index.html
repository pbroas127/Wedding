Q & A

Everything you need to know

What is the dress code?+
Garden party chic. Think flowing, romantic, and comfortable enough for cobblestone paths. We encourage soft florals, linen suits, and anything that makes you feel like you belong in the Italian countryside. Please avoid white, ivory, or champagne out of respect for the bride.
What should I wear to the Welcome BBQ on April 18?+
The Welcome BBQ theme is Something Blue Before I Do. Wear your best shade of blue! Think navy linen, dusty blue sundresses, powder blue blazers, or any hue of blue you love. Smart casual to cocktail attire works perfectly.
Will the ceremony be indoors or outdoors?+
The ceremony will be held outdoors in the gardens of Casale Doria Pamphilj, a breathtaking 17th century estate in the Roman countryside. The reception dinner will move inside to the grand hall. April in Rome is typically warm and sunny with highs of 65 to 72°F.
Do I need a passport? Any visa info?+
Yes — make sure your passport is valid for at least 6 months beyond April 20, 2027. US citizens do not need a visa for stays under 90 days in Italy. Book flights and accommodations as early as possible. Check your government's travel advisory site for the most current entry requirements.
How do I get to the Casale from the airport?+
Rome Fiumicino Airport (FCO) is just 5km from Casale Doria Pamphilj, making it one of the easiest arrivals imaginable. A quick taxi or rideshare from the airport gets you straight to the villa in under 10 minutes. All guests are staying on site for the full weekend so once you arrive, you are home.
Can I take photos during the ceremony?+
We're asking guests to keep the ceremony phone free so you can be fully present in the moment and so our photographer can capture you without screens in the way. Feel free to snap away during the cocktail hour and reception. Our hashtag is #OliviaAndEthan2027.
When should I book my flights?+
As soon as possible! Spring in Rome is peak travel season. We recommend booking by September 2026 at the latest. April in Italy is magical so we encourage you to arrive early or stay after and explore the city. Check our Things to Do page for our favorite spots.
What is the RSVP deadline?+
Please RSVP by February 1st, 2027. This gives us enough time to finalize catering, seating, and logistics with the venue. Head to the RSVP page to submit your response — we can't wait to celebrate with you!

Travel

Everything you need to plan your Roman holiday

Your stay is covered. All guests are hosted at Casale Doria Pamphilj for the full weekend. Just book your flight to Rome Fiumicino (FCO) and we take care of the rest. We recommend booking flights by September 2026 for the best availability.

Getting There

Flying In

Fly into Rome Fiumicino (FCO), Leonardo da Vinci International Airport. It is just 5km from the Casale, making it one of the easiest arrivals you will ever have. Direct flights from most major US cities are available via ITA Airways, Delta, and United.

Airport to Villa

From FCO, a quick taxi or rideshare drops you at the Casale in under 10 minutes. Casale address: Via Onorato Occioni, 12, 00054 Fiumicino. Rideshare apps FREE NOW and Uber both operate at FCO. It truly could not be easier.

Your Home for the Weekend

Casale Doria Pamphilj

A stunning 17th century estate set in the Roman countryside with 25 independent apartments on site. You will wake up to vineyards, gardens, and Italian skies every morning of the weekend. This is your home base and it is extraordinary.

What Is Included

Accommodations for the full weekend, all three events (Welcome BBQ, Wedding, Farewell Pizza), meals, and drinks are fully covered by Olivia & Ethan. Arrive Thursday or Friday, stay through Monday. All you need to bring is yourself.

On Site Amenities

The Casale features a pool, wine cellar, gardens, and Bar Emporio on the grounds. Spend the days between events exploring the estate, tasting local wine, and soaking in the Italian sun before the celebrations begin.

Extend Your Trip

Rome deserves more than a weekend! We highly encourage you to arrive early or stay after. The city is just 30 minutes away. Head to our Things to Do page for our personal guide to the best of Rome.

Things to Do

Our personal guide to Rome & beyond

Rome is a city that rewards the wanderer. These are our favorite spots — the places we've fallen in love with on our own trips. We hope you fall for them too.

Must-See

The Colosseum

Book tickets in advance — the line without a booking can be hours. Spring light makes the stone glow golden. Go at opening time for the best experience.

Must-See

Trastevere

Our favorite neighborhood. Cobblestone streets, ivy-covered buildings, and the best aperitivo bars in the city. Perfect for an evening wander and dinner.

Art & Culture

Borghese Gallery

Book well in advance — entry is strictly timed. Bernini's sculptures are among the most breathtaking things you'll ever see in person. Allow two hours minimum.

Food & Drink

Roscioli

The best cacio e pepe in Rome, full stop. Also a wine bar and deli. Make a reservation — it books up weeks ahead. Worth every bit of the wait.

Food & Drink

Gelato at Fatamorgana

Artisanal gelato with unusual flavors like basil, ginger, and pink pepper. Multiple locations across Rome — don't skip this one.

Food & Drink

Campo de' Fiori Market

Every morning there's a lively market — grab fresh fruit, local produce, and flowers. Then settle into one of the surrounding bars for a proper espresso.

Day Trip

Ostia Antica

An ancient Roman port city just 30 minutes from Rome — and far less crowded than Pompeii. Incredibly preserved with stunning mosaics and open-air ruins.

Day Trip

Frascati Wine Region

30 minutes from Rome in the Castelli Romani hills. Rolling vineyards, panoramic views, and the local white wine is delightful. Perfect for a half-day escape.

Hidden Gem

Aventine Keyhole

Peek through the Knights of Malta keyhole for a perfectly framed view of St. Peter's Dome. Free, quick, and utterly magical. Don't miss it.
